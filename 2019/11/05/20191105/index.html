Why I want to learn hacking

裴昌庙街一号院2号楼
2019-11-05
hacking

First of all, I would like to declare that I am not a hacker. Calling me a scripkiddie is a compliment for me. For me, hacking represents an attitude of curisity For me, hacking represents an attitude of curiosity of life. When I was a kid, I have curiosity of everything: Why airplane can fly in the sky? Why my father can ride a bike with two wheels but not falling down? Why red apples are sweet and green apples are sour? When people grow up, they may know the answers from school, books or internet, or they may lost their curiosity. But I am still curios.

In some people’s eyes, hackers are a bunch of cool guys who wear black hoodie and can break into government’s databases with few commands. I used to be one of them, but I believe one thing that hackers are human beings.

My first hacktivity happened when my 4th grade in primary school. I was studying on some computer competition, which was nothing related to computer – the competition required us to play a game of simple mathematics (24 Game) and vocabulary. We need to practice to at another larger primary school with computer lab, and I noticed that the game results were saved in a text file. I opened it with notepad and edited results. I didn’t expect anyone would notice it and it was practice result only. A teacher from the computer lab found it when he tried to do reconciliation of our practice results and he noticed that a student’s result was 500 million while the average score was about 200+. My parents were so surprised that I had capability to do it and bought me a new pen as reward.

Hacking is not limited to blackhats who steal credit card information or dump database illegally. Everyone can be hackers. In my view, lawyers are hackers. They need to read the regulations line by line to find loophole to utilize, which is exactly same as what cyber hackers do, who read source code or test application to find vulnerabilities that can be abused. Find a bug and use it, that is what hackers do.

I told people I took OSCP for fun, and no one believed it. I don’t expect myself to have any career achievement in cybersecurity area, but I really enjoy the moment when I prompt a root shell.
